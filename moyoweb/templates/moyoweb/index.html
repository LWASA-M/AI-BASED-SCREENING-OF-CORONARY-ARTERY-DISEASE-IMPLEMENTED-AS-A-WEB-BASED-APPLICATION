Why Coronary artery disease screening Using Artificial Intelligence?

Screening for Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) in individuals at risk is crucial for early detection and management. Here is why we employ AI for screening purposes:-

  • Traditional methods of assessing CAD such as medical history,physical examination,electrocardiogram and coronary angiography mostly rely on basic clinical metrics which makes them miss early signs of the development of CAD
  • Conventional CAD risk assesment procedures often apply on broad population statistics which might not be accurate for individual patients
  • Acces to high quality screening tools and special care varies significantly across different regions where mostly rural areas lack advanced risk assesment tools making it hard to detect CAD at early stages.

MoyoGuard AI aids healthcare providers in early detection and decision-making, but final assessments and treatments should always be guided by qualified medical professionals.

How can I Improve my Coronary artery disease score?

  • Eat a heart-healthy diet.
  • Get regular aerobic exercise (at least 150 minutes per week)
  • Lose weight (if needed) and maintain a healthy weight
  • Get help to quit smoking and avoid secondhand smoke
  • Manage conditions like high blood pressure and diabetes

What risk factors are associated with Coronary artery disease?

  • Smoking
  • Obesity
  • Hypertension
  • Family History
  • Age
  • Sex
  • Diabetes status

MoyoGuard AI helps to determine the pattern in these risk factors, how they interact to cause CAD and make accurate risk assesment

Our AI screening tool helps to detect Coronary artery disease at early stages to guide healthcare support and further assessment.However, it is important to note that this tool does not replace the expertise of a doctor. While the AI provides valuable insights, it should be used as a complementary tool. The final diagnosis and treatment decisions should always be made by a healthcare professional who can consider the full clinical context.

Facts

Did you Know?

In 2010, One in four deaths was caused by Coronary artery disease making a 25% contribution to all deaths.

By 2030, the deaths attributable to cardiovascular diseases are expected to reach 25 million deaths annually

In sub Saharan Africa the deaths due to coronary artery disease were 138,308 deaths in 1990 which rose to 258,939 deaths in 2013,causing an increase by 87% of deaths.

The number of cardiovascular disease patients increased from 2.5 million in 2017 to 3.4 million patients in 2022 making a 9.4% increase in cardiovascular disease cases in Tanzania.

Coronary artery disease is a slowly progressing disease involving long asymptomatic stages for almost a decade followed by symptomatic stages such as experiencing angina, during the long period of asymptomatic stage it is difficult for an individual to notice the presence of the disease, sometimes the symptomatic stage is not manifested leading to sudden deaths.
